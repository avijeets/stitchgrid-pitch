The problem

Sourcing still runs on
brokers, Excel, and email.

  • A brand that wants to make 2,000 hoodies spends 6 to 9 months finding, vetting, and negotiating with factories.
  • The work is done by sourcing agents and brokers. It is manual, opaque, and buries a 10 to 20% margin inside the quote.
  • Factories price the goods, not the journey. Freight, duties, and tariffs are an afterthought to them, but landed cost is the one number a brand can actually plan around.
It is labor, not software. Reading a messy brief, matching it to capable factories, and reconciling inconsistent quotes is exactly the language work AI is now good at.

The status quo

Three outdated ways to find a supplier today.

01 · Back-channel agents

Who you know. Relationship-based middlemen who take 10 to 20% and answer to no one. No transparency, and it all dies if your contact does.

02 · Alibaba

A directory, not a workflow. Mostly Chinese factories, no real reviews, no milestone pricing, no help with samples, freight, payment, or QC.

03 · Trade shows

Inconsistent and expensive. A flight, a booth fee, and a few days a year to maybe meet the right factory, then back to email and hope.

And not one of them helps after the handshake. Samples, fittings, tech-pack revisions, freight, payment, QC: you are on your own.
